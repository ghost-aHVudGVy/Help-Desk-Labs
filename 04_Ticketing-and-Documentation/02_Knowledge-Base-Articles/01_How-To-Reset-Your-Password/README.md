**Category:** User Account Management  
**Audience:** All employees  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-07

---

## Overview

This article provides step-by-step instructions for resetting your password in both **Active Directory (Windows domain)** and **Microsoft 365** accounts. Following these steps ensures your account remains secure while restoring access quickly.

---

## Resetting Your Windows Domain Password

1. Press **Ctrl + Alt + Delete** on your workstation.  
2. Click **Change a password**.  
3. Enter your **current password** in the first field.  
4. Enter your **new password** in the next field.  
5. Confirm your **new password** by entering it again.  
6. Click **Enter** to apply the change.

> **Password Requirements:**  
> - Minimum 8 characters  
> - At least one uppercase letter  
> - At least one lowercase letter  
> - At least one number  
> - At least one special character (!@#$%^&*)  

---

## Resetting Your Microsoft 365 Password

1. Go to [https://passwordreset.microsoftonline.com](https://passwordreset.microsoftonline.com)  
2. Enter your **work email address** and follow the prompts.  
3. Choose a verification method (email, phone, or authenticator app).  
4. Enter the verification code you received.

## Common Issues & Troubleshooting

| Issue | Possible Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| **Account locked after multiple attempts** | Too many incorrect password attempts | Contact IT support to unlock your account. |
| **Password not accepted** | New password does not meet complexity requirements | Ensure it includes uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. |
| **Cannot access Microsoft 365 reset page** | Network or VPN issues | Connect to the corporate network or VPN and try again. |

---

## Notes

- Always choose a password that is **unique** and **not reused** from other accounts.  
- If you suspect your account was compromised, **contact IT support immediately**.  
- Updating your password may require you to **re-enter credentials** on devices like Outlook, Teams, or mobile apps.
